Working under pressure

December 21st, 2022

by Robert Whisenhunt

These past few months have been very rough on me. My mother and I weren't able to afford a new lease back in August
and had to put most of our belongings in a storage unit and move into a hotel room that the church helped us afford.
Early September, my mother had a bad fall and broke her left hip. I was the one that found her as I returned to the hotel
room from taking the laundry to the laundry mat at the hotel. During this time, I was just into the fall semester of my online
collage courses. My mother insisted I stay at the hotel, finish the laundry, and do my schoolwork; so I stayed.

She was in the hospital over night and had surgery the next day. Following her surgery, she knew that rehab would be
the better option, but because of her previous history at rehab earlier this year, she refused to go and insisted on coming
back to the hotel. My stepsister brought her back to the hotel and said she hated this was put on me and wished me luck.
So, that night it was me taking care of my mother, despite her being back so soon. I had to help her to the bathroom
a total of seven times throughout the night and morning. The seventh trip in which she didn't make it and made a mess
on the bathroom floor. She told me to call 911 to take her back to the hospital, and they came to take her back to the hospital.

Once back at the hospital, she finally agreed to go to a rehab center to get the professional help she needed. While she was
in rehab, I continued to do the best I could to continue with my schoolwork, but also had the stress of finding a new place for
us to call home, as well as take care of the cat and myself. Stress became so bad that I developed issues with my blood pressure
and my doctor put me on blood pressure medicine. It started to become difficult to focus on my schoolwork as weeks went by with
mom in rehab, learning new complications that started going on with her. I started to fall behind on some of my schoolwork, which
made me more stressed because that was more work to do on top of everything else I was dealing with at the time.

November came, and mom was sent to the hospital to get tests done to figure out what was causing the complications she was having
such as fluids in her abdomon and legs. They discovered that the source of the fluids was her liver, and that she was at end-stage
cirrhosis and had her sent to a hospice care facility. This made focusing on schoolwork even harder, but I was doing the best I could
to catch up on what I was behind on. My sister would pick me up and take me to visit mom, which seeing her in her condition was
difficult.

Friday, November 25th, my sisters and I met a church member at the hospice care faclity as the church member was offering to give
her what he called an "end of life blessing". Knowing it would be difficult to experience, I wanted to assist by placing my hands
on mom's head as he conducted the blessing. I had asked for some one on one time with my mom prior to the blessing. While I was
with her, I wanted her to hear it from my own mouth that I would be okay and that she didn't have to worry about me. I told her
I had many friends already showing their support and that I was in good hands. She was unable to talk, and was half awake, but she
was able to nod her head a few times while I was talking to her. It was a difficult moment, but a moment I was thankful to have.

The next day, my stepsister called me and told me the nurses deemed mom as non-responsive and that she was showing color change
and that it wouldn't be long at that point. The next day, November 27th, my sister woke me up a little after 6pm and was crying.
Without her telling me, I just knew, but I said "I'm guessing she passed huh?..." and my sister, while crying, say yes. She herself
witnessed our mother take her last breath. I have been a mess since, as depression from grief has taken me over for the past couple
of weeks. My instructors have been very nice and understanding through this rough time I am experiencing, and one of them is giving me
a last minute chance to hopefully clutch a passing grade. Which is what I am doing right now, spending all night and morning to get
everything done that he has requested and hope for the best.

It has been difficult having to type this up and re-live everything I've gone through these past couple of months, but if it means
succeeding in this course that I worked so hard on getting through and was doing so well before everything went down hill, then it is
worth it. I've been going at it since 2am this morning. As I am typing this, it is just barely after 9am. I have until 11am to have
everything submitted. During this time, I've learned how to create a wireframe for the homepage of this blog web page. I didn't have
time to make a wireframe for the two child pages, so I kind of wung it from there, but I can honestly say that I have learned a lot
from this course, and I am very thankful for my instructor in giving me this last minute chance.

Thank you for taking the time to read this post. I am going to do the remaining two assignments now, and hope for the best and then
put my focus on my mother's funeral services after this is all done and see what the winter semester has in store for my future.
If everything goes well and I pass this course, I will just have two more courses left to complete my Computer Programming Certificate.
Which is why I'm taking this last minute chance that I have been given. It's been a lot of pressure, and even got anxious once, but
I am managing and will get everything done on time.

Thank you for reading this, thank you Mt. Dew Code Red for helping me stay awake, and have a Merry Christmas.
